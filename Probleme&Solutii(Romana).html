1 Zgomot si trosnituri pe linia telefonica

Zgomote si trosnituri statice pe linia telefonica care apar atunci când utilizati telefonul, este orientativ din cauza filtrelor incorect montate

  • Asigurati-va ca ati instalat corect toate filtrele.
  • Este important ca la fiecare dispozitiv-telefon sa fie montat un filtru. Nu uita masinile de fax si boxele, deasemenea, au nevoie de un filtru.
2. ADSL se deconecteaza atunci când se face apel telefonic sau se primeste
  • Filtre instalate incorect - verifica daca filtre de control sunt instalate corect.
  • Filtre de defecte - înlocuieste unul câte unul, sa identific care filtru este defect.
  • Defectului este pe linie - putin probabil, dar posibil în cazul în care orice altceva a fost eliminat.
3. ADSL functioneaza numai atunci când telefonul este utilizat
  • Verificati filtrele si cablurile interne.
  • Verificati pentru zgomot pe linia care ar putea ajuta la identificarea problemei, de a face un "test de linie de liniste"
  • Daca este asa - Posibil un defect pe linia de telefon actuala, probabil din cauza cablurilor uzate sau apa de pe linie. Pot exista momente când un cablu este doar usor avariat / corodat si nu prezinta nici o problema imediata, cu toate acestea, prejudiciul este suficient pentru a creste rezistenta pe linie. Atunci când linia telefonica este în uz, genereaza un mic curent electric care este necesar pentru a transporta semnalul vocal. Acest curent ajuta la reducerea rezistentei pe linie si poate fi suficienta pentru a "reduce decalajul", doar suficient pentru a transporta semnalul ADSL. Acest lucru este cunoscut ca un Big Open sau High Resistence Fault.

Powercycle

The easiest and most effective troubleshooting step. What we mean by “powercycle” is turning everything off at the wall — modem, router (if you have one) and computer. Leave it off for a minute and then turn everything back on. Wait a few minutes for everything to start up, then check to see if you can browse websites or download your e-mail.

Isolation test

One of the fastest ways to find a line or filtering problem is performing what’s called an “isolation test”.

An isolation test means:

  1. Connecting the ADSL modem directly to a telephone wall socket (i.e., no line filter) with a telephone lead no more than 3 metres in length.
  2. Disconnecting every other device connected to any other telephone wall socket in the premises. This includes double adapters and extension leads.

Once you’ve completed the above two steps, powercycle the modem. Some modems take a while to successfully connect to the ADSL service, so please be patient. If the light goes solid, all well and good – move on to the one by one test. If it hasn’t gone solid within five minutes, there are an additional two tests you need to try:

  • With the modem in isolation, replace the telephone lead connecting the modem to the telephone wall socket with another lead no more than 3 metres in length.
    With the modem in isolation, relocate the modem (no need to move your computer!) to a different wall socket.
  • With the above two steps, you’re trying to determine if a faulty telephone lead or wall socket are the cause of the problem. In each case, you need to powercycle the modem and wait for at least five minutes to see if the ADSL light will go solid. If it does, you’ve found (at least part of) the cause of the problem and you should move on to the one by one test. If it doesn’t, please contact the tech support team for further assistance.

One by one test

A one by one test should be performed after you’ve successfully managed to get your ADSL modem to connect up while doing an isolation test. Quite simply, a one by one test is the process of connecting devices back on to the telephone line one by one.

After you connect each device (making sure that you’ve connected it using an ADSL line filter), test the Internet connection for a good few minutes to ensure that it continues to work properly. After you’re satisfied that it still works, connect the next device, test the Internet connection, and so on.
